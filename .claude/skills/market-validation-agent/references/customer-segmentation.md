# Customer Segmentation & ICP

Define the Ideal Customer Profile *before* sizing the market or scanning competitors. Everything downstream depends on knowing exactly who you're solving for.

## What an ICP is

An ICP is a narrow, specific description of the customer most likely to (a) feel the pain acutely, (b) pay for the solution, and (c) be reachable. Vague ICPs ("small businesses", "developers", "millennials") produce vague research. Good ICPs are concrete enough that you could name three real people or companies that fit.

## Two distinct roles

For B2B and B2B2C, separate the **buyer** from the **user**. They are often different people with different concerns:
- A SOC manager (buyer) signs the security contract; the security engineer (user) lives in the tool daily.
- A school district (buyer) buys edtech; teachers (users) actually use it.

Misalignment between buyer and user is one of the most common reasons B2B products fail to retain. Surface this explicitly when relevant.

## What to produce

For each major segment (start with 1–3 max), write up:

**Firmographics or demographics**
- B2B: company size band, industry, geography, tech stack, revenue range
- B2C: age, income, location, life stage, occupation

**Psychographics & behavior**
- What jobs are they trying to get done (Jobs-to-be-Done framing)?
- What are they currently using to get the job done?
- What triggers them to look for a new solution?

**Pain intensity**
- How often does the pain occur? (daily / weekly / monthly / yearly)
- How costly is the pain in time, money, or risk?
- Is it a "hair on fire" problem or a nice-to-have?

**Reachability**
- Where do they hang out online? (specific subreddits, forums, conferences, publications)
- Who do they trust for recommendations?
- What's the realistic acquisition channel?

## Research approach

Search for evidence of real people fitting your candidate ICP and validate that they actually exist and have the pain.

Useful query patterns:
- `[role/persona] [problem] reddit`
- `[role/persona] daily workflow`
- `[industry] pain points [current year]`
- `how do [role] currently [job-to-be-done]`
- `[role] survey [current year]` (LinkedIn surveys, industry surveys often reveal pain points)

Read at least 5 substantive user voices (forum posts, reviews, blog posts, podcast transcripts) that confirm or refute the assumed pain. Capture verbatim quotes — they show up later in the demand-signals step and the final report.

## Red flags to surface

- The team behind the idea cannot name three real people who fit the ICP
- The ICP description shifts depending on which part of the pitch you're hearing
- The buyer and user have conflicting incentives
- The reachable population is much smaller than the assumed TAM would suggest
- The pain is real but ranks #7 on the user's priority list — no budget will be allocated

## Output for this step

A short ICP section (200–400 words) covering: segment name, firmographics/demographics, JTBD, pain intensity, current alternatives, reachability, and 2–3 verbatim user quotes with source URLs. This section is dropped directly into the final report.
